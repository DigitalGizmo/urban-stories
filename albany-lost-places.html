Displaced People

The Reaction to Redevelopment

The State announces decision to seize 98.5 acres in downtown Albany for construction of new state office complex. City of Albany wakes up to learn the news—some that their homes now belong to the State and they will soon have to move

We might use the frontpage of the Times Union and/or Knick News, editorial cartoons as illustrations.

Man-on-the-street interviews

 

 

John Johnson: “These houses are old. I think it would be swell to tear these buildings down.”

James Strawn agreed that “tearing down ‘these 50 and 60-year-old buildings might make for some decent places in which to live.’”

George Kemp: “This would be a pretty good deal if they included some housing projects too.”
