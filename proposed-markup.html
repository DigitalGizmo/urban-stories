Displaced People

The Reaction to Redevelopment

The State announces decision to seize 98.5 acres in downtown Albany for construction of new state office complex. City of Albany wakes up to learn the news—some that their homes now belong to the State and they will soon have to move

Man-on-the-street interviews

John Johnson, James Strawn, and George Kemp

John Johnson: “These houses are old. I think it would be swell to tear these buildings down.”

James Strawn agreed that “tearing down ‘these 50 and 60-year-old buildings might make for some decent places in which to live.’”

George Kemp: “This would be a pretty good deal if they included some housing projects too.”

Lillian Pierce

"How is it going to help downtown? Naturally, we don’t want to move. Even the news may scare my people away. And they’ve been coming her for years.

"Everyone's talking about this. Even the grocer up the street on Hawk Street. He wants to move out now instead of waiting."

John Sgarlata

"Thirty years ago this market was open day and night. The Menands Market opened. That killed this. Then Park & Shop opened. That and the automobile killed the rest of the farmer trade. I’m an old guy. I’ll be 67 in August. I’m retired practically already and my son, Frank, will be in charge."
